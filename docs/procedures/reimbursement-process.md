# Reimbursement Process

Status: Needs Finance Review

## Guiding Principle

Routine expenses within approved ministry budgets should be easy to submit and reimbursed promptly after the approval required by the Spending Authority Policy, with monthly Finance Committee review.

Expenses outside budget, over budget, unusual, recurring, or within an escalated approval level require pre-approval before purchase whenever possible.

## Routine Reimbursement Process

Use this process for ordinary ministry expenses that are within an approved budget and have the required prior approval.

### Ministry Leader / Requester Steps

1. Confirm the expense is for an approved church or ministry purpose.
2. Confirm the expense is within the approved ministry budget.
3. Obtain and retain evidence of the approval required by the [Spending Authority Policy](../policies/spending-authority-policy.md).
4. When practical, use the church's current valid tax-exempt purchasing documentation. Ask the assigned church contact when eligibility or use is unclear.
5. Make the purchase and keep the receipt or invoice.
6. Submit the reimbursement request within 30 days. If that is not possible, include a written explanation for the appropriate reviewer.
7. Include:
   - Requester name.
   - Date of purchase.
   - Vendor.
   - Amount.
   - Ministry, fund, or budget category.
   - Brief explanation of church purpose.
   - Receipt or invoice.
   - Evidence of required approval.
8. If the requester is also an assigned approver, obtain review from another authorized person. The requester may not be the only approver.

### Bookkeeper / Financial Administrator Steps

1. Receive the reimbursement request and documentation.
2. Confirm the request includes the required information.
3. Confirm a receipt or approved missing-receipt explanation is included.
4. Confirm the ministry, fund, or budget category is identified.
5. Confirm that evidence of the approval required by the Spending Authority Policy is included.
6. Check whether the expense appears routine, within budget, timely, and within the documented approval.
7. If complete and compliant, prepare the reimbursement for payment according to the approved payment process.
8. Record the transaction in the financial system.
9. Attach or file supporting documentation with the transaction record.
10. Add the reimbursement to the monthly Finance Committee review packet.
11. Flag any late, unclear, incomplete, unusual, outside-budget, over-budget, or improperly approved request for independent review before payment.

### Finance Committee Steps

1. Review reimbursement activity monthly.
2. Confirm reimbursements are documented and coded properly.
3. Review any missing receipt explanations.
4. Review late, unusual, over-budget, outside-budget, recurring, or escalated reimbursements.
5. Identify policy exceptions, repeated late submissions, or repeated documentation problems.
6. Decide whether any follow-up, policy change, ministry leader communication, or budget adjustment is needed.

## Expenses Requiring Pre-Approval

Pre-approval is required before purchase whenever practical for:

- Expenses outside the approved budget.
- Expenses that would cause a ministry or category to exceed budget.
- Expenses that reach an escalated approval level in the Spending Authority Policy.
- Unusual or non-routine expenses.
- Recurring subscriptions, contracts, or commitments.
- Equipment, technology, furniture, or capital items requiring escalated approval.
- Travel, lodging, mileage, conference, or training expenses unless already approved.
- Emergency purchases, unless the adopted emergency process authorizes immediate action.
- Any expense that may require Finance Committee or congregational approval.

## Exception Process

Use this process for outside-budget, over-budget, unusual, recurring, emergency, or escalated-approval expenses.

1. Requester or ministry leader identifies the need before purchase whenever possible.
2. Requester submits an explanation with:
   - Purpose.
   - Estimated amount.
   - Budget category or proposed funding source.
   - Reason the expense is needed.
   - Timing or urgency.
3. The approver required by the Spending Authority Policy reviews the request.
4. Approval or denial is documented before purchase when possible.
5. If approved, requester completes the purchase and submits receipt/documentation.
6. Bookkeeper processes the reimbursement only after approval and documentation are complete.
7. Finance Committee reviews the exception at the next monthly review.

Emergency exception:

- Emergency authority may be used only when delay would endanger people, cause or worsen significant property damage, interrupt essential church operations or services, or prevent an urgent ministry need from being addressed.
- The authorized emergency approver may act only within the adopted policy and may not use the exception as a routine bypass.
- The requester must submit documentation as soon as practical.
- The Finance Committee should review the emergency expense at the next meeting.

## Missing Receipt Process

Receipts should be required whenever possible. Missing receipts should be exceptions, not normal practice.

1. Requester notifies the Bookkeeper that the receipt is missing.
2. Requester provides a signed written explanation including:
   - Date of purchase.
   - Vendor.
   - Amount.
   - Ministry or budget category.
   - Church purpose.
   - Reason the receipt is unavailable.
3. Requester and Bookkeeper obtain available secondary evidence, such as an invoice, email confirmation, packing slip, bank/card record, or vendor reprint.
4. Treasurer or assigned approver reviews the missing-receipt explanation before payment.
5. If approved, Bookkeeper records the reimbursement and files the explanation with the transaction.
6. Missing-receipt reimbursements are listed for monthly Finance Committee review.
7. Repeated missing receipts by the same person or ministry should be addressed by Finance Committee or the appropriate supervisor/committee.

## Late Submission Process

1. Requester explains in writing why the request was not submitted within 30 days.
2. The appropriate independent reviewer approves or denies the timing exception.
3. Bookkeeper retains the explanation and approval with the transaction.
4. Repeated late submissions are included in the monthly Finance Committee review.

## Monthly Review Packet Items

The reimbursement section of the monthly Finance Committee packet should include:

- Total reimbursements paid during the month.
- Reimbursements by ministry or budget category.
- Any outside-budget or over-budget reimbursements.
- Any reimbursements requiring escalated approval.
- Missing receipt explanations.
- Unusual or recurring reimbursements.
- Late requests and requests pending approval or documentation.
- Recommended follow-up items.

Needs Finance Review: Finance Committee should confirm the proposed 30-day submission period, approved reviewer roles, Spending Authority Policy alignment, emergency authority, tax-exempt-document custody, late-request exceptions, and missing-receipt approval standard.
