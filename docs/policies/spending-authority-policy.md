# Spending Authority Policy

Status: Needs Finance Review

## Purpose

This policy defines who may approve church spending and when additional review, committee action, or congregational approval is required.

The dollar levels below are proposed thresholds for Finance Committee and bylaw review. They are not adopted authority until the proper church body approves them.

This document is part of the [Financial Authority Policy Package](financial-authority-policy-package.md). Terms, thresholds, and approval paths should be reviewed across the package so the policies do not conflict.

!!! note "Congregation Final Authority"
    The congregation approves the annual budget and remains the final authority where the KBC Constitution and Bylaws, budget, policy, amount, or church practice require a vote. Congregational notice, quorum, and voting requirements are governed by the Constitution and Bylaws.

## Proposed Spending Authority Matrix

Use the total annual or contractual commitment, not merely one payment or invoice, when determining the approval level. Outside-budget and over-budget spending also requires the exception or budget-amendment approval described below.

| Total Purchase Or Commitment | Proposed Prior Approval | Review Status |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| Up to `$250` | Pastor | Proposed Finance Committee recommendation; confirm an independent approver when the Pastor is the requester or beneficiary. |
| `$251-$1,000` | Pastor and Finance Committee Chair | Proposed Finance Committee recommendation. |
| `$1,001-$3,000` | Finance Committee | Proposed Finance Committee recommendation. |
| More than `$3,000` | Congregation at a properly called business meeting | Needs bylaw review and congregational approval before becoming policy. |

The matrix does not give the Bookkeeper / Financial Administrator authority to initiate or approve spending merely because that person records or processes a transaction.

## General Spending Rules

- Every purchase must serve a valid church purpose.
- Purchases should normally fit within the approved annual budget.
- Inclusion in the budget does not automatically authorize every individual purchase; the applicable approval level still applies.
- Approval authority does not authorize spending outside the approved budget unless the required exception, funding decision, budget transfer, or budget amendment has also been approved.
- No employee, volunteer, ministry leader, committee, or other person may commit church funds without the required prior approval.
- Personal purchases may not be made with church funds.
- The person requesting or benefiting from a payment may not be the only approver.
- Every purchase must have sufficient documentation for the Bookkeeper, Treasurer, and Finance Committee to verify the purpose, budget category, approval, and amount.
- Approval, payment, recording, reconciliation, and review duties should be separated so one person does not control the full transaction.
- Deacon counsel may be helpful for major or sensitive matters, but it does not replace Finance Committee review or congregational approval where required.

## Budget Exceptions

Outside-budget or over-budget spending must be identified before church funds are committed whenever practical.

The request should document:

- Church or ministry purpose.
- Expected amount and full commitment.
- Budget category and proposed funding source.
- Reason the existing budget is insufficient.
- Required approval under the proposed matrix.
- Whether a budget transfer, budget amendment, or congregational vote is required.

Finance Committee should review the budget impact and recommend congregational action whenever the Constitution and Bylaws, approved budget, policy, amount, or church practice require it.

## Quotes And Competitive Bids

The following requirements are proposed for Finance Committee review:

- Under `$1,000`: reasonable pricing is expected.
- `$1,000-$3,000`: obtain at least two quotes when practical.
- More than `$3,000`: obtain at least three written quotes when practical.
- When competitive quotes are not practical, document the reason. Examples include an emergency, a sole-source vendor, compatibility requirements, or a lack of qualified vendors.
- Quote requirements do not replace the required spending approval.

Finance Committee should decide how quotes and approved exceptions will be retained with the purchase record.

## Contract Authority

- No employee, volunteer, ministry leader, committee, or other person may sign a contract or create a binding recurring obligation on behalf of KBC unless specifically authorized.
- Approval should consider the full annual or contractual commitment, including setup costs, recurring payments, renewal terms, cancellation terms, and termination penalties.
- Contract approval must follow the same approved-budget, spending-escalation, exception, and congregational-approval requirements that apply to other spending.
- Finance Committee approval does not replace congregational approval when the Constitution and Bylaws, approved budget, policy, amount, or church practice require a church vote.
- The authorized signer and the approval evidence should be documented before the contract is signed.

## Emergency Purchases

Emergency authority may be used only when delay would:

- Endanger people.
- Cause or worsen significant property damage.
- Interrupt essential church operations or services.
- Prevent an urgent ministry need from being addressed.

An emergency is not a routine way to bypass normal budget or approval procedures. The authorized emergency approver and any emergency spending limit must be confirmed by Finance Committee and checked against the Constitution and Bylaws.

The person acting under emergency authority must document the circumstances, amount, vendor, church purpose, and reason normal approval could not be obtained. Finance Committee should review the purchase promptly after the fact, and the matter should be reported or presented to the congregation when required.

## Conflict Of Interest

Any officer, committee member, employee, ministry leader, or volunteer with a financial interest in a proposed purchase must:

- Disclose the interest before the decision.
- Abstain from approving or influencing the purchase.
- Ensure the disclosure and independent approval are documented.

Questions about related parties, compensation, tax treatment, or legal obligations should receive professional review when appropriate.

## Review And Revision

The Finance Committee should review this policy at least annually and whenever budget practice, approval authority, or church governing documents change.

Needs Finance Review: The proposed approval matrix, quote requirements, budget-exception process, contract authority, emergency approvers and limits, conflict-of-interest process, documentation, and review cadence must be confirmed before adoption.

Needs Bylaw Review: Congregational approval triggers, contract authority, budget-amendment authority, notice, quorum, voting thresholds, and the final adoption path must be checked against the KBC Constitution and Bylaws and established church practice.
