# Financial Authority Policy Package

Status: Needs Finance Review

## Purpose

This package gives the Finance Committee one coordinated place to review how KBC authorizes purchases, reimburses people, controls payment methods, handles exceptions, evaluates contracts, and returns major matters to the congregation.

It is a review package, not an approved policy. The proposed dollar thresholds and controls below require Finance Committee review. Congregational authority, contract authority, budget amendments, notice, quorum, and voting requirements also require review against the Constitution and Bylaws before adoption.

!!! note "Congregation Final Authority"
    The congregation approves the annual budget and remains the final authority for major non-budgeted expenses and other financial matters where the Constitution and Bylaws, adopted policy, budget, amount, or church practice require a vote.

## Source Material Informing This Revision

The July 2026 `source-materials/prior-proposals/finance/Kingsville Baptist Church Purchasing Policy Guidelines.pdf` proposal was reviewed and distributed into the existing modular policies. Its useful approval, documentation, quote, contract, emergency, card-security, and conflict-of-interest provisions are presented here as draft review input, not as adopted policy.

The proposal's specific quorum and supermajority language was not incorporated. Those requirements must come from the current Constitution and Bylaws. Its Missouri sales-tax statement was also not adopted as permanent policy.

## Core Control Model

1. The congregation approves the annual budget and matters reserved for church action.
2. The Finance Committee maintains financial-control policies, monitors the budget, reviews exceptions, and recommends matters requiring congregational action.
3. Authorized ministry or operational leaders may make purchases only within the approved budget, assigned authority, and applicable approval level.
4. The Treasurer / Financial Officer reviews financial activity, coordinates reporting, and helps carry out Finance Committee direction as an elected officer.
5. The Bookkeeper / Financial Administrator records and processes properly authorized activity but does not create spending authority.
6. No person should request, approve, process, reconcile, and report the same transaction without independent review.
7. Contracts and recurring commitments are evaluated on their full annual or contractual obligation, not merely one monthly payment.

## Proposed Purchase Approval Matrix

These are proposed Finance Committee recommendations, not approved authority.

| Total Purchase Or Commitment | Proposed Approval | Approval Path To Confirm |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| Up to `$250` | Pastor | Finance Committee recommendation; independent approval needed when the Pastor is requester or beneficiary. |
| `$251-$1,000` | Pastor and Finance Committee Chair | Finance Committee recommendation. |
| `$1,001-$3,000` | Finance Committee | Finance Committee recommendation. |
| More than `$3,000` | Congregation at a properly called business meeting | Bylaw review and congregational approval. |

Outside-budget and over-budget requests require the applicable exception, funding decision, budget transfer, or budget amendment in addition to the matrix approval.

## Operating Guidance To Confirm

Purchasers should use the church's current valid tax-exempt documentation when practical. This is an administrative purchasing practice, not a permanent statement about a particular state's tax law or the church's eligibility for every transaction. Finance Committee should assign custody of the documentation and obtain tax or legal guidance when needed.

## Recommended Review Sequence

1. Confirm the current bylaw, congregational-vote, annual-budget, and contract-authority requirements.
2. Agree on the control model before finalizing individual dollar amounts.
3. Complete the decision worksheet in a Finance Committee meeting.
4. Check that the three policies and related procedures use the same definitions and approval paths.
5. Ask the Treasurer, Bookkeeper, ministry leaders, and Pastor to test the proposed workflow against common situations.
6. Obtain professional review where tax, legal, insurance, banking, accounting, or employment questions arise.
7. Bring any policy or threshold requiring congregational approval to the church with a concise recommendation.
8. Record the effective date, owner, approval body, and annual review date after adoption.

## Adoption Checklist

- [ ] Finance Committee has recorded its recommendation on each proposed threshold and control.
- [ ] The Constitution and Bylaws have been checked for budget, contract, congregational-vote, notice, quorum, and voting requirements.
- [ ] The approval matrix has been tested against routine, large, outside-budget, over-budget, recurring, and emergency examples.
- [ ] Competitive quote requirements and documented exceptions are practical and clear.
- [ ] Contract authority identifies who may approve and who may sign.
- [ ] Conflict-of-interest disclosure, abstention, and independent approval are documented.
- [ ] Reimbursement, card, and monthly-review procedures match the policy language.
- [ ] Separation of duties prevents the Bookkeeper or any other person from controlling the entire transaction.
- [ ] Items reserved to the congregation are presented through the proper church process.
- [ ] Professional review has been obtained where needed.
- [ ] The final adoption body, effective date, owner, and next review date are recorded.

## Questions To Test Before Adoption

- Can a ministry leader identify the required approval before committing church funds?
- Can the Bookkeeper see proof of approval before processing a transaction?
- Is a recurring commitment evaluated on its full contractual obligation?
- Were the required number of quotes obtained, or is a practical exception documented?
- Can a person with a financial interest influence or approve the purchase?
- Can Finance Committee identify outside-budget, over-budget, card, reimbursement, contract, and emergency exceptions during monthly review?
- Is the Treasurer providing meaningful oversight without becoming the only gatekeeper?
- Are payment cards and credentials individually attributable and protected from sharing?
- Does the package clearly return required matters to the congregation without inventing quorum or voting rules?
